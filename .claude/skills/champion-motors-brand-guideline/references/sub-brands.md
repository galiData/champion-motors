# Sub-brand governance — the six marques

Champion Motors imports and distributes six Volkswagen Group marques in Israel. Each is a global brand with its own binding corporate-identity manual, owned in Wolfsburg / Ingolstadt / Martorell / Mladá Boleslav — not in Israel.

Champion's brand equity is in **the relationship**: importing, retail, service, financing, trust. The marques' equity is in **the product**. Keeping those separate is what makes both work.

## The governing question

> **Is the subject of this piece a car, or is it Champion?**

If the subject is a car — a model, its features, its launch, its price — the marque owns the visual identity and Champion endorses. If the subject is Champion — the service network, the company, the people, the financing offer, careers — Champion owns it.

Everything else follows from that.

## Layer matrix

| Layer | Typical deliverables | Visual identity | Voice | Champion's role |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Champion corporate** | About, careers, CSR, group news, investor material, internal comms, service-network campaigns | Champion palette + Ploni | Champion voice, fully | Owner |
| **Marque product** | Model launches, model pages, product campaigns, product social, brochures, showroom product POS | **Marque manual** — its colors, its type, its photography rules | Marque's global tone, adapted to Hebrew market | Importer endorsement only |
| **Co-branded retail & service** | Service centers, financing, private leasing, trade-in (AutoDeal), Champ app, aftersales, parts | Champion leads; marque logos in a respectful lockup | Champion voice | Owner, with marque presence |

Ambiguous cases and how they resolve:

| Case | Layer | Why |
|---|---|---|
| "Book a service for your Škoda" | Co-branded retail | Service is Champion's operation; Škoda is the context |
| "The new Audi Q6 e-tron has arrived" | Marque product | The car is the subject |
| "Champion Motors named a top employer" | Champion corporate | The company is the subject |
| "Private leasing on the VW T-Roc" | Co-branded retail | The *financing product* is Champion's; the car is context |
| "CUPRA brand experience at the showroom" | Marque product | CUPRA's brand world is the subject — its manual governs the space |
| Careers post for a VW showroom sales role | Champion corporate | Champion is the employer |

## Per-marque character notes

These are orientation notes, not substitutes for the manuals. They help you pitch copy correctly when writing Hebrew market adaptation.

| Marque | Positioning | Hebrew name | Tone when writing product copy |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Volkswagen** | Accessible quality, the mainstream default, "the people's car" | פולקסווגן | Warm, sensible, family-aware. Confident without prestige posturing. |
| **VW Commercial** | Working vehicles — Transporter, Caddy, Crafter | פולקסווגן מסחריות | Practical, businesslike, reliability- and TCO-focused. Speak to a business owner. |
| **Audi** | Premium, technology leadership, *Vorsprung durch Technik* | אאודי | Precise, restrained, technology-forward. Understatement is the register. Never gushing. |
| **SEAT** | Youthful, Mediterranean, design-led, value | סיאט | Energetic, warm, design-conscious. The most playful of the six — still never hard-sell. |
| **CUPRA** | Performance and rebellion, spun out of SEAT, copper accents, emotive | קופרה | Bold, contemporary, emotional. Highest-energy voice; still no discount shouting. |
| **Škoda** | Clever practicality, "Simply Clever", space and value | סקודה | Down-to-earth, smart, human. Leads with usefulness and thoughtful detail. |

CUPRA is where tone discipline is easiest to lose. Its brand world is genuinely bold and emotive — that is *not* a license for `!!!` or manufactured urgency. Bold voice, calm commercial conduct.

## Lockups and endorsement

**Endorsement line.** On marque-product material, Champion appears as importer, quietly:

> `יבואן רשמי בישראל — צ'מפיון מוטורס`
> (Official importer in Israel — Champion Motors)

Placement is typically the footer or the legal/contact block. It should never compete with the marque's own lockup for hierarchy.

**Co-branded lockup.** On Champion-led retail and service material:

- Champion mark is primary — larger, first in the reading order (which in RTL means the rightmost position).
- Marque logos appear as a secondary row, at consistent optical size, separated by a hairline or generous spacing.
- Preserve each marque's mandated clear space — those minimums come from the marque manuals and are not negotiable.
- Never recolor a marque logo. Not to Champion Blue, not to navy, not to monochrome unless that marque's manual explicitly permits a mono version.
- Never place a marque logo on a busy photograph or a low-contrast background.

**Never do:**
- Combine a marque logo with the Champion mark into a single new composite lockup
- Alter proportions, add effects, outline, or rotate any marque mark
- Imply Champion designed, engineered or manufactured a vehicle
- Use one marque's assets in material about another marque

## What to do without a marque manual

You will frequently be asked to produce launch or product material without the marque's current CI files. Do not fill the gap by guessing — a plausible-looking wrong Audi red is worse than an obvious placeholder.

Do this instead:

1. **Write the copy and structure fully.** Message hierarchy, headlines, body, CTA, section flow — none of that is blocked by missing visual tokens.
2. **Use neutral placeholders** for marque-owned visual decisions: `--marque-primary`, `--marque-accent`, `[MARQUE WORDMARK]`, `[Marque display face]`.
3. **Flag it explicitly** in the deliverable:

   > ⚠️ Visual treatment is placeholder only. Final colors, typography and logo usage must follow the current [Marque] Corporate Identity manual before publication.

4. **List what's needed** so the user can go get it: the CI manual version, the approved logo files, the marque's Hebrew-market type permissions.

This is genuinely more useful than an invented palette, and it's honest about what was and wasn't verified.

## Common failure modes

| Failure | Why it happens | Fix |
|---|---|---|
| Champion Blue applied to an Audi campaign | Skipping Step 0; "it's a Champion project" | Identify the layer first, every time |
| Marque logo recolored to fit a layout | Design convenience | Adjust the layout, never the mark |
| Composite "Champion × Audi" logo invented | Trying to express partnership visually | Use the endorsement line instead |
| Hard-sell tone on a CUPRA piece | Mistaking bold brand energy for sales pressure | Bold voice, calm commercial conduct |
| Fabricated marque hex codes | Not wanting to return incomplete work | Placeholders plus an explicit flag |
| Champion's 70-year heritage claimed for a car | Blending corporate and product stories | Heritage is Champion's story, not the vehicle's |
