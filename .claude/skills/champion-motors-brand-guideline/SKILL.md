# Champion Motors Israel — Brand Intelligence

You are producing work for **Champion Motors Israel (צ'מפיון מוטורס)**, part of the **Allied Group**. The company is the official Israeli importer and distributor for six Volkswagen Group marques and operates ~40 service centers nationwide.

Everything in this skill is grounded in the live brand (championmotors.co.il) — the colors and typeface were extracted from the production stylesheet, not invented. Treat them as facts, not suggestions.

---

## Step 0 — Always ask: whose brand am I in?

This is the most important decision in any Champion Motors task, and the easiest to get wrong.

Champion Motors is an **importer**. It does not own Audi, VW, SEAT, CUPRA or Škoda — those are Volkswagen Group marques with their own binding global brand manuals. Producing an "Audi ad in Champion blue" is a real compliance failure, not a stylistic quibble.

Before writing or designing anything, place the work in one of three layers:

| Layer | What it covers | Whose visual identity governs |
|---|---|---|
| **Champion corporate** | The importer itself: careers, About, group news, service network as a whole, investor/CSR, dealer-facing internal comms | **Champion** — palette and type in this skill |
| **Marque product** | A specific car: a new Tiguan, an Audi Q6 e-tron launch, CUPRA campaign, model pages, product social | **The marque's global manual.** Champion appears only as importer endorsement |
| **Co-branded service** | Service centers, financing, leasing, trade-in, the Champ app, retail experience across marques | **Champion leads**, marque logos appear as a respectful lockup |

If the layer is ambiguous, say so and ask — one sentence — rather than guessing. Getting this wrong wastes the whole deliverable.

When you are in a **marque product** layer and don't have that marque's manual, do not fabricate its colors or fonts. Write the copy and structure, use neutral placeholders for marque-specific visual tokens, and flag clearly: *"Visual treatment must follow the current Audi Corporate Identity manual — placeholders used here."*

Full governance matrix, logo lockups, and per-marque character notes: **`references/sub-brands.md`**

## Brand DNA

**Essence:** A trusted mobility partner bringing global automotive excellence to Israeli customers.

**Positioning:** International automotive innovation combined with local trust, professionalism and human relationships. Champion is the bridge — German engineering on one side, an Israeli customer who wants to be treated like a person on the other.

**Official mission (from the company):** Represent and lead — responsibly and sustainably — the Volkswagen Group brands in Israel; provide customers and employees a unique experience, enjoyment, belonging and pride, while creating value for shareholders and contributing to society.

**Heritage:** Founded over 70 years ago by Aaron Gottwirt ז"ל, who built the business alongside humanitarian work. The Aaron Gottwirt Foundation continues that legacy. This matters: longevity and social contribution are earned assets — reference them where relevant, never inflate them.

**Values** (the five working values, mapped to the company's formal value set):

| Value | Means in practice | Formal anchor |
|---|---|---|
| **Trust** | Transparency in price, timeline and condition. Say what you'll do; do it. | Integrity & trustworthiness; loyalty & commitment |
| **Excellence** | Certified expertise, continuous improvement, no "good enough" | Achievement & initiative; professionalism |
| **Human connection** | A named person, not a queue number. Respect and personal consideration. | Humanity |
| **Innovation** | Electrification, digital service, new mobility — framed as useful, not futuristic | Mission: leading responsibly |
| **Responsibility** | Sustainability, safety, community, employer conduct | Mission: contributing to society |

**Personality:** Professional · Premium · Confident · Human · Innovative · Reliable

Confident is not loud. Premium is not expensive-sounding. The brand is the senior specialist who explains things clearly and doesn't need to raise their voice.

## Voice and tone

**Clear · Professional · Warm · Inspirational — never aggressive sales language.**

That last clause is a hard rule, and it is the rule most often broken. Champion sells premium European cars to a market that is saturated with discount shouting. The brand's differentiation *is* the refusal to shout. Hard-sell copy doesn't just feel off-brand — it actively destroys the trust the whole positioning rests on.

### The four voice tests

Run these on any copy before you hand it over:

1. **Would a professional say this out loud to a customer's face?** If it only works in a banner, cut it.
2. **Is there a claim I can't substantiate?** "The best in Israel" needs a source or it goes.
3. **Is the pressure coming from the customer's need or from our quota?** Urgency the customer actually has (winter tires before rain) is service. Urgency we manufacture is pressure.
4. **Is there a human in this sentence?** Not always required, but if a whole page has no person in it, the warmth is missing.

### Banned patterns

Never use, in Hebrew or English:

- Manufactured scarcity: `רק היום` · `הזדמנות אחרונה` · `מהר לפני שייגמר` · "act now" · "last chance"
- Discount shouting: `מבצע ענק` · `חיסול` · `במחיר הזול בשוק` · "unbeatable price" · "cheapest"
- Pressure CTAs: `לחץ כאן עכשיו!!!` · "buy now!!!"
- Multiple exclamation marks, ALL-CAPS shouting, or `!!!` anywhere
- Superlatives without evidence: "the best", `הכי טוב בארץ`

Legitimate commercial information is fine — a real price, a real financing rate, a genuine date-bounded program. State it calmly and factually. The difference is between *informing* and *pressuring*.

### Rewrite examples

**Campaign headline**

> ✗ `מבצע ענק! רק היום — טיגואן החדש במחיר הזול בשוק! אל תפספסו!!!`
>
> ✓ `הטיגואן החדש הגיע לאולמות התצוגה. נשמח להכיר לכם אותו מקרוב — בתיאום נסיעת מבחן במרכז הקרוב אליכם.`

Why: the second gives the reader a reason and a next step, and treats the car as worth meeting rather than worth dumping.

**Service page**

> ✗ `השירות הכי טוב בארץ! בואו עכשיו!`
>
> ✓ `40 מרכזי שירות ברחבי הארץ, עם טכנאים מוסמכי קבוצת פולקסווגן. נתאם לכם טיפול במועד שנוח לכם.`

Why: a verifiable fact (40 centers, certified technicians) does the persuading, and the close is an offer of convenience rather than a command.

**Dealer communication**

> ✗ `חובה לעמוד ביעדים החודש, אחרת יהיו השלכות.`
>
> ✓ `יעדי הרבעון מאתגרים, ואנחנו כאן כדי לתמוך. צוות ההדרכה זמין לליווי אישי בכל אולם.`

Why: "Human connection" is an internal value too. Dealers who are treated as partners transmit that to customers; dealers who are threatened transmit that as well.

## Visual identity — core

### Palette

Extracted from the production stylesheet. Contrast ratios are computed against white and dictate where each color may legally carry text.

| Token | Hex | Role | On white |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Navy** | `#020755` | Primary brand surface. Headers, footers, dark sections. The authority color. | 18.0:1 ✓ AAA |
| **Deep Blue** | `#00335C` | Headings on light backgrounds, secondary UI, borders, menus | 12.9:1 ✓ AAA |
| **Champion Blue** | `#1760F0` | *The action color.* Links, primary buttons, focus rings, interactive states | 5.3:1 ✓ AA |
| **Sky** | `#539FFF` | Hover/active states — on dark surfaces or large text only | 2.7:1 ✗ never small text on white |
| **Signal Red** | `#FF0036` | Price emphasis, genuine time-bounded programs, alerts. **Sparing.** | 4.0:1 — large text only (18pt+/14pt bold) |

Neutrals: `#FAFAF9` `#FCFBF9` `#F5F5F5` (surfaces) · `#CED5DF` `#D9D9D8` `#CCCCCC` (borders, never text) · `#707070` (4.9:1 ✓) `#5F5F5F` (6.4:1 ✓) `#505050` (secondary text) · `#0F0F0F` (primary body text)

**Signal Red discipline.** Red is the one color that can push the brand into discount-shouting territory. It reads as urgency, and the voice rules forbid manufactured urgency — so the visual system has to hold the same line. Use it for a price figure or a real deadline. Never as a decorative fill, a section background, or the theme of a campaign. If a layout has red in more than one role, cut it back.

Ready-to-paste CSS custom properties: **`assets/brand-tokens.css`**

### Typography

The brand typeface is **Ploni** — a Hebrew type family, used across the site in Ultralight, Regular, Demibold and Bold.

- **Headlines:** Ploni Demibold or Bold. Large sizes carry the premium feel; tight leading, generous space around.
- **Body:** Ploni Regular, comfortable line height (1.5–1.7 for Hebrew — Hebrew needs a little more air than Latin).
- **Ultralight:** display sizes only. It disappears below ~32px and fails accessibility as body text.
- **Fallback stack:** `'ploni', 'Assistant', 'Heebo', system-ui, sans-serif` — Assistant and Heebo are free Hebrew faces with similar proportions when Ploni isn't licensed for the surface.

Never substitute a decorative or condensed face. The premium European feeling comes from restraint and spacing, not from expressive type.

### Layout and imagery

- **Clean and spacious.** Whitespace is the primary premium signal. When a layout feels cramped, remove elements rather than shrinking them.
- **Photography:** high-quality, natural light, real environments. People in frame wherever the subject allows — this is a human-centered brand. Avoid stock-looking staged smiles and avoid cars floating on gradient voids.
- **Human-centered storytelling:** the customer, the technician, the family — the car is the enabler, not always the hero.
- **Restrained motion:** subtle, purposeful. No aggressive transitions.

Full spec — type scale, spacing system, grid, photography direction, iconography, logo usage and clear space: **`references/visual-identity.md`**

## Hebrew and RTL

Default output language is **Hebrew**. Produce English only when the user asks, or when the audience is clearly international (group/investor materials).

RTL is not a mirror-image afterthought — get these right or the work reads as imported:

- Set `dir="rtl"` and `lang="he"` on the document root.
- Use **CSS logical properties** — `margin-inline-start`, `padding-inline-end`, `inset-inline-start` — instead of left/right. This is what makes a layout genuinely bidirectional rather than hand-flipped.
- **Directional icons mirror** (arrows, chevrons, back/next, progress). **Logos, and icons depicting real objects, do not** — a car icon still faces the way it faced.
- **Numbers, prices, model names and Latin brand names stay LTR** inside Hebrew text: `אאודי Q6 e-tron`, `החל מ־189,000 ₪`. Let the browser's bidi algorithm handle it; wrap in `<span dir="ltr">` only when a string renders wrong.
- Hebrew has no case. Emphasis comes from **weight and size**, never from capitalization tricks — and never from stretching or condensing letterforms.
- Punctuation and quotation marks follow Hebrew convention; use the geresh/gershayim (׳ ״) correctly in abbreviations like צ'מפיון.

## Validation

Before delivering anything, check it against this list. When the deliverable is a file, run the script — it catches the mechanical failures faster and more reliably than reading.

```bash
python .claude/skills/champion-motors-brand-guideline/scripts/validate_brand.py <file> [more files...]
```

It flags off-palette hex colors, banned sales phrases in Hebrew and English, exclamation-mark abuse, contrast violations, non-brand font stacks, and missing RTL attributes in HTML. Exit code is non-zero when there are errors, so it drops into a pre-commit hook or CI cleanly.

**Manual checks the script can't make:**

- [ ] **Layer is correct** — Champion identity is not applied to marque-owned product material
- [ ] **Claims are substantiated** — every superlative or statistic has a real source
- [ ] **A human is present** — the piece shows or addresses people, not just machinery
- [ ] **Red is disciplined** — Signal Red appears in at most one role, tied to a real fact
- [ ] **The voice tests pass** — would a professional say this out loud to a customer's face?
- [ ] **Whitespace is generous** — nothing is cramped to fit more in
- [ ] **RTL is native** — logical properties, correct mirroring, LTR numbers intact
- [ ] **Tone is warm, not soft** — clear and confident, without pressure

If something fails, fix it rather than noting it. The point of the checklist is a clean deliverable, not an annotated flawed one.
